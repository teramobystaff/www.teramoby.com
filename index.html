Agentic identity infrastructure

Agents need identities people can govern.

As software agents start acting across apps, teams need to know which agent is acting, what it is allowed to do, and who is accountable for each decision.

The gap

Human identity systems were not designed for autonomous workers.

01

Unknown actors

Agents often borrow human credentials, service accounts, or API keys without a durable identity of their own.

02

Loose authority

Permissions are hard to scope when an agent can plan, call tools, and take multi-step actions on behalf of a team.

03

Weak accountability

When something changes in production, finance, support, or data systems, teams need an audit trail that maps action to owner.

What we are building

teramoby is exploring the identity layer for agentic software.

We are at the beginning: mapping the problem space, talking with builders, and designing primitives for agent identity, delegation, policy, and verifiable activity.
